Obama For America Ohio Lawsuit<p>On July 17, 2012, Obama for America and the Democratic National Committee filed suit against the Attorney General and Secretary of State of Ohio.&nbsp; In their lawsuit, the Obama campaign and the DNC argue it is "arbitrary" and unconstitutional to provide three extra days of early, in-person voting to military voters and their families.&nbsp; At least 20 times in their legal papers, they argue that there is no good reason to give special flexibility to military voters &mdash; and that this policy adopted by the Ohio legislature is so wrong it is unconstitutional.<p>We disagree with the basic premise that it is "arbitrary" and unconstitutional to give three extra days of in-person early voting to military voters and their families, and believe it is a dangerous and offensive argument for President Obama and the DNC to make.&nbsp; It is not only constitutional, but commendable that the Ohio legislature granted military voters and their families this accommodation.&nbsp; It is despicable for the Obama campaign to challenge Ohio's lawful decision.<p>But this lawsuit is not about us.&nbsp; Last week, respected military groups intervened in the Obama campaign's lawsuit.&nbsp; They argue that it is absolutely constitutional to give military voters special flexibility in voting, and that it is offensive for the Commander-in-Chief's political campaign to argue otherwise.&nbsp; They correctly point out that there is good reason to grant military voters special flexibility; members of the military—including those stationed here within the United States—are subject to restrictions, uncertainties, and risks that often make it more difficult for them to vote.&nbsp; We agree with these military groups.<p>The Obama campaign may not like the early voting policy that the Ohio legislature set.&nbsp; This does not mean the policy is unconstitutional, and it certainly does not mean that a federal court should be permitted to remake it.</span><hr noshade="noshade" size="1"><span class="displaynotes"><i></i></span><hr noshade="noshade" size="1">
